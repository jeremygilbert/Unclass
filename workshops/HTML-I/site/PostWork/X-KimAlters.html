Darkwing Duck

This animated action adventure telecision series was produced by The Walt Disney Company and ran from 1991-1992 on The Disney Channel. It featured the eponymous duck superhero whose alter ego is mild-mannered single quacker Drake Mallard. It is the only direct spin-off of DuckTales.

Premise

Darkwing Duck tells the adventures of the titular superhero, aided by his sidekick and pilot Launchpad McQuack. In his secret identity of Drake Mallard, he lives in an unassuming suburban house with his adopted daughter Gosalyn, next door to the bafflingly dim-witted Muddlefoot family. Darkwing struggles to balance his egotistical craving for fame and affection against his desire to be a good father and help do good in St. Canard.

Main characters

  • Darkwing Duck / Drake Mallard (voiced by Jim Cumings) - Average citizen by day and St. Canard's resident superhero by night. He possesses a mix of courages altruism and a gigantic arrogance and ego, two drives that constantly clash during his adventures - in "Just Us Justice Ducks."
  • Launchpad McQuack (voiced by Terry McGovern) - Darkwing Duck's sidekick, originally from DuckTales. He refers to Darkwing as "DW" and Darkwing sometimes refers to him as "LP" in return. He's Darkwing's self-proclaimed biggest fan.

The critics: Los Angeles Times

    With his long bill, Lone Ranger mask, billowing cape and outsized slouch hat, Darkwing looks suspiciously ike Daffy Duck in his swashbuckler outfit from Chuck Jones' "The Scarlet Pumpernickel" (1950). The use of Warners-style takes--quick looks at the camera, flickering eyebrows, hammy mock-heroic poses--only emphasizes the similarities. But Daffy was funny, and this inept crime fighter who describes himself as ". . . the terror that flaps in the night . . . the winged scourge that pecks at your nightmares" isn't.

- Charles Solomon
